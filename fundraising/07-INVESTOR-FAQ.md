## About the Opportunity

### Q: What problem are you solving?

**A:** [Brief problem statement with specific impact]

**Example:** *"Healthcare providers waste $50K/year on IT support for password resets. 40% of IT tickets are password-related. Current solutions are too expensive ($15/user/month) and complex for small clinics."*

**Tips:**
- Start with cost/time impact
- Use specific numbers
- Show this is a real, painful problem

### Q: Why now?

**A:** [Technology enablers + market timing]

**Example:** *"Three changes: (1) WebAuthn now supported by all browsers (2) FHIR API adoption makes integration 10x easier (3) HIPAA fines up 300%, driving compliance investment."*

**Tips:**

### Q: What's your unfair advantage?

**A:** [Domain expertise / Technical advantage / Network]

**Example:** *"10 years at Epic building auth for 200+ hospitals. Deep HIPAA expertise and relationships with 50+ clinic IT directors ready to beta test. Would take competitor 3-5 years to replicate."*

**Tips:**
- Be specific (not "we work hard")
- Show what's hard to replicate

## Competition

### Q: Who are your competitors?

**A:** [Direct competitors + status quo]

**Example:** *"Direct: Okta ($15/user, too expensive), Auth0 (not HIPAA-compliant). Indirect: Microsoft Entra (complex). Status quo: 70% use spreadsheets - our biggest competitor."*

**Tips:**
- List 3-4 direct competitors
- Don't forget status quo

---

### Q: What if [big competitor] enters your space?

**A:** [Why you'll still win]

**Example:** *"Microsoft has innovator's dilemma - they make money on Azure/Office, not standalone healthcare SMB auth. We have 2-3 year head start on EHR integrations. By then we'll have 500+ customers with high switching costs."*

**Tips:**
- Show you've thought about this
- Explain your moat/defensibility

---

### Q: How are you different from [Competitor X]?

**A:** [Specific differentiation]

**Example:** *"vs. Okta: (1) Pre-built EHR integrations (2) HIPAA-compliant out of box (3) $300/mo vs $750/mo (4) 15-min setup vs 3-month implementation."*

**Tips:**
- Use specific feature/price comparisons
- Show deep competitor understanding

## Traction & Validation

### Q: How many customers do you have?

**A:** [Actual numbers + pipeline]

**Example Pre-Revenue:** *"Pre-revenue with MVP in development. 75 customer interviews, 5 signed LOIs for pilot programs in 3 months. 3 clinics committed to $300/mo."*

**Example Early Revenue:** *"12 paying customers = $4K MRR ($48K ARR). Pipeline: 25 qualified leads, 8 in active sales. 45-day sales cycle."*

**Tips:**
- Be honest about stage
- Show forward momentum

---

### Q: What's your CAC?

**A:** [Actual or projected CAC + payback]

**Example:** *"Target CAC: $3K (6 months founder time + $500 marketing). At $500 MRR with 85% margin, 7-month payback. Beats 12-18 month benchmark."*

**Tips:**
- Show your math
- Include payback period
- Compare to benchmarks

---

### Q: What have you learned from customers?

**A:** [Key insights that shaped product]

**Example:** *"Three insights: (1) 85% cited password resets as #1 pain - validated focus (2) Want biometric auth, not hardware - changed approach (3) Only pay if EHR integration - made it priority."*

**Tips:**
- Show you listen to customers
- Demonstrate product-market fit understanding
