## What is Due Diligence?

**Due diligence** is the process where investors verify your claims and assess risks before investing.

**Typical timeline:**
- Friends & Family: Minimal due diligence (1-2 days)
- Angels: Light due diligence (1-2 weeks)
- VCs: Thorough due diligence (2-8 weeks)

**Your goal:** Have everything organized and ready to share instantly

## Due Diligence Checklist

### 2. Intellectual Property

## Due Diligence Timeline

### Week 1: Initial Review
- Investor reviews pitch deck, executive summary
- Asks clarifying questions
- Requests data room access

### Week 2-3: Deep Dive
- Investor reviews all documents
- Schedules calls with founders
- May request customer references
- Technical investors may review code

### Week 4: Legal Review
- Investor's lawyers review legal docs
- May request changes to SAFE terms
- Negotiate final deal points

### Week 5-6: Closing
- Final agreements signed
- Wire transfer received
- Cap table updated
- Investor updates begin

**Total: 4-8 weeks** (can be faster for small angels, slower for VCs)

## Best Practices

### Before First Investor Meeting

1. **Organize data room** (even if no one's asked yet)
2. **Update all documents** (ensure dates, numbers are current)
3. **Run self-audit** (go through this checklist yourself)
4. **Fix obvious issues** (clean up cap table, file missing docs)
5. **Practice answers** (to common diligence questions)

### During Due Diligence

1. **Respond quickly** (within 24-48 hours)
2. **Be transparent** (disclose problems upfront, don't hide)
3. **Stay organized** (track all requests in spreadsheet)
4. **Keep founder aligned** (if multiple founders, ensure consistent answers)
5. **Don't oversell** (be realistic about risks and challenges)

### After Closing

1. **Send thank you** (to investor and their team)
2. **Set update cadence** (monthly investor updates)
3. **Update data room** (keep current for next round)
4. **Learn from process** (note questions for next fundraise)
